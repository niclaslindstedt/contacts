type: Added
title: Dated backups you can browse, download, and restore
---

Take timestamped snapshots of your whole address book — every contact, folder,
photo, and attachment — packed into a compressed `.zip`. From **Settings →
Storage → Backups** you can **Download backup (.zip)** straight to disk or
**Restore from file…** to roll the current document back to a downloaded
snapshot (a destructive replace, so it confirms first).

When a file-backed backend is connected — a picked local folder, Dropbox, or
Google Drive — **Browse backups** opens a manager over a `backups/` folder on
that backend: take a fresh snapshot with **Back up now**, then download, delete,
or **restore** any of them. Restoring files the current data as a safety-net
snapshot first, so it's always undoable, and the restored copy syncs back up as
the live document. Snapshots are named with their timestamp and contact/folder
counts so the list reads at a glance.

Backups are per-namespace, and the compressed archive keeps everything at full
fidelity independent of which backend externalises photos day to day. The
off-device manager is hidden while encryption at rest is on, since a plaintext
snapshot must not land beside your encrypted copy — download and restore from
disk still work.
